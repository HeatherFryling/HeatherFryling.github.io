Save the Bees!!


What You Can Do to Save the Bees

Now that you know all about the plight of the bees, we’re sure you want to help. Here are some easy ways you can make a difference.

Avoid insecticides

Pesticides kill more than pests. They also kill bees. Avoid using pesticides in your home garden. Source.

Plant a bee-friendly garden:

Flowering plants provide the nectar that makes bees thrive. Here are some easy to grow plants that are tasty for both you and the bees. To support the bees, don’t cut the plants before they flower.

  • Rosemary
  • Lavender
  • Mint
  • Strawberry
  • Raspberry
  • Basil

    • Source.

Provide water

Bees need water to cool their hives and to raise their young. You can put a bird bath or other water source in your yard to help the bees. Source.

Buy local honey

By supporting a local beekeeper, you support local bees. Most farmers markets have beekeepers eager to sell you delicious honey from healthy hives.

Preserve natural habitat

Wild plants provide healthy food and shelter for bees. Avoid picking wildflowers or trampling plants when you wander in wild areas. Washington wild plants particularly beloved of bees include:

  • Lupine
  • Goldenrod
  • Oregon grape
  • Salal

Remember that if you let native plants grow in your yard, your own yard can also be a lovely bee habitat. Dandelions, for example, are a very important early spring food source for bees when they’re just coming out of hibernation. Save the dandelions, save the bees. Source.

Eat organic

Just as buying healthy honey supports healthy bees, buying healthy plants supports healthy bee habitat.
